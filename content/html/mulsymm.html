Ok, what's that above?

I'm confident you know about multiplication tables. The board above just show a number of them arranged as rows to shape a square. The number of tables shown (and thus the square size) can be set by the Size slider. It can be (mathematically) any size, but the board above is limited to 30 for practical reasons.

When you select a number by means of the Show multiples of slider, the board will highlight those numbers in the board that are multiples of it. E.g. if you choose 5: 0, 5, 10, 15, etc. will be highlighted. The main (and only?) purpose of this useless machine is to show that multiples are regularly scattered across the multiplication tables, often in the shape of visually interesting patterns.

For improved wonder, a second number can be added. As you may expect the board will then highlight in different colours the multiples of either of them. Whenever a cell holds a number that's a multiple of both of them, it'll be highlighted with a nice gradient.

As a side effect, you may be get some ideas for you bathroom or swimming pool tiling :P . Numbers can be hidden by the Show numbers slider if you'd rather focus on the merely visual.
